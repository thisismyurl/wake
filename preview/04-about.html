Est. 1962

Sixty-four years on the water

Harbour & Sea began as a racing club. It became something broader: a community of people who take the water seriously, who write about it as well as sail on it, and who believe that the sea demands more than a GPS waypoint.

We are based at the south end of Georgian Bay, on Lake Huron, with an active racing program on Lake Erie and an annual cruise of the North Channel that has run uninterrupted since 1974. Our journal publishes accounts from members and correspondents — voyages, race reports, seamanship notes, and the occasional essay on why the water matters.

Our History

1962

The Founding

Twelve boats, a borrowed dock at Penetanguishene, and a constitution written on graph paper. The club held its first race on the June long weekend and has not missed a year since.

1974

First North Channel Cruise

Eleven boats departed Penetanguishene for Killarney in what became the club's defining annual tradition. The cruise has run every summer since, interrupted only by two seasons during which the travel-lift was out of commission.

1998

The Journal Begins

The newsletter became a proper journal: offset-printed, forty pages, distributed to 380 members. It published voyage accounts, seamanship notes, and race reports through 2018 when it moved online.

2026

The New Site

The journal moves to its current home. 640 members, three race series, and an annual cruise that draws boats from as far away as Lake Ontario and Lake Superior.
